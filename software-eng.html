Term Definition
Software Engineering The discipline of designing, implementing, and maintaining software systems.
Integrated Development Environment (IDE) A software suite that consolidates basic tools required for software development.
Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) A professional organization dedicated to advancing computing as a science and profession.
Purpose of ACM To promote computing innovation and the exchange of information.
Benefits of ACM Membership Access to publications, professional networking, and career development resources.
Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) The world's largest technical professional organization dedicated to advancing technology.
Purpose of IEEE To foster technological innovation and excellence for the benefit of humanity.
Software Life Cycle: Development Phase Includes requirements analysis, design, implementation, and testing.
Unified Modeling Language (UML) A standardized modeling language for visualizing system design.
Software Testing Techniques include glass box testing, black box testing, beta testing, and alpha testing.
Intellectual Property Law Legal protection for software through licenses and disclaimers.
Best Defenses Against Software Liability Claims Robust documentation, testing, and adherence to industry standards.
