U.S. Department of State

The U.S. Department of State (DOS) is responsible for advancing American foreign policy and promoting global peace. Established in 1789, the DOS has a wide range of duties that include protecting American citizens abroad, maintaining diplomatic relations, and fostering international cooperation.

  • Mission: To advance U.S. interests worldwide.
  • Key Functions: Diplomacy, consular services, and international advocacy.
  • Notable Programs: The Fulbright Program, Human Rights Report, and more.
